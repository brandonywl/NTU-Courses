Students will learn the chemical changes that take place during storage and processing of agricultural crops and food. Special attention will be paid to endogenous enzymes as present in the agricultural raw materials from which most foods are prepared. In addition, during the laboratory classes, students will learn the design of experiments and the analysis of the composition of food products. Food technologists should be aware of the importance of the various chemical and enzymatic processes and should be able to estimate their relevance. To practice this part of food chemistry, quantification of specific reactions will be practiced in computer cases.
