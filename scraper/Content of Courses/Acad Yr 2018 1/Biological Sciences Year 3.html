BS0001 BIOLOGY & SOCIETY 3.0 AU
Not available to Programme: BMS(2004-2010), BS(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), BS(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
This course aims to enlighten you about the importance of studying biology and to deliver an unbiased view of the impact of biology on human society. You will study about various ecosystems such as rainforests and mangrove forests and the social and econonomical values they give to mankind. You will learn about some biological discoveries and innovations that are used in our society in the field of forensics and sports. You will learn about the potential benefits and threats that bio-technology, particularly genetic engineering, brings to mankind and the environment. You will learn about viruses that are harmful to man. You will learn the biology of our human body and bacteria that have benefited mankind in improving health and well-being.

BS3004 CANCER BIOLOGY & THERAPY 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: BS203 & BS821 OR
BS2004
Mutually exclusive with: BS308
The course will survey the genes and gene products which control cellular proliferation, and the genetic changes that lead to the various stages of cancer. You will apply this information by studying real or imagined experiments and proposing hypotheses to explain the results. You will think broadly about the molecular mechanisms regulating cell function and how these are assessed experimentally. You will apply your understanding of the molecular mechanisms regulating cancer cells to understand how therapies can be developed to target cancer. Learning will be accomplished through a combination of traditional lectures, online learning and lab practicals.
 
BS3014 BIOLOGICAL FOUNDATIONS OF BEHAVIOR 3.0 AU
This course aims to explore an evolutionary analysis of animal behavior. You will work in teams to unravel case studies in class and while completing assignments. You will analyse behavior from the vantage points of natural selection vis-a-vis alternative explanations. The course strives to bring into sharp relief the influence of evolution on biological processes and aims to foster critical examination of behavioral evidence with an equipoised interpretation of conflicting views. In the words of Theodosius Dobzhansky,"Nothing makes sense except in the light of evolution."

BS3023 REGULATORY CONTROL OF HEALTHCARE PRODUCTS & MEDICAL DEVICES 3.0 AU
This 13-week programme provides a complete and concise treatment of the key considerations in the development and regulation of healthcare products. The main area of concentration is in medical devices and is divided into four parts. The course begins with a historical introduction into the healthcare products industry and the start of regulatory controls. It then moves into the core principles of healthcare products regulations and expands into the methodologies of ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements. This includes going through the fundamentals of Good Manufacturing Practices, quality management systems, product risk classification, risk management and the different regulatory systems in the world. At the end of this course, you should have a clear understanding of healthcare products regulations and able to apply these requirements in product development. You will also be required to perform casework to test their ability to apply these concepts in real scenarios.

BS3338 UAEB II - EFFECT OF ANTI-MITOTIC DRUGS ON CANCER CELLS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: BS1007
In this one-week workshop, you will examine cancer cells treated with anti-mitotic cancer drugs using different molecular and cell biology experimental techniques. Through a series of experiments, you will appreciate how various scientific hypotheses are developed and how experiments are designed to address these hypotheses. In addition, you will understand the mechanism on how anti-mitotic treatments affect cancer cells' viability in cancer therapy. Furthermore, you will develop skills in data acquisition and analysis, scientific communication in terms of oral presentation and report writing.
 
BS3342 UAEB II -ROLE OF ACTIN CYTOSKELETON REGULATORS IN METASTASIS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: BS1007
This course aims to introduce the techniques used in the analysis of epithelial mesenchymal transition, a precursor to metastasis. During this one-week workshop, you will analyse the effect of inhibition of actin cytoskeleton regulators on epithelial mesenchymal transition using different molecular and cell biology experimental techniques. You will gain better insight into how scientific hypotheses are formulated based on existing data and how experiments are designed to test these hypotheses. You will appreciate the role of actin cytoskeleton in many cellular processes such as cell migration during metastasis. You will also develop skills in data acquisition and analysis, and scientific communication in terms of oral presentation and report writing.

BS3345 UAEB II - MICROBIAL BIOTECHNOLOGY & SYSTEMS BIOLOGY 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: BS2014
This one-week course aims to provide undergraduate students with opportunities for in-depth learning and training in microbial biotechnology and systems biology, focusing on experimental design, skillsets, data acquisition and analyses of bioreactors and RNA sequencing-based transcriptomics. In this course, you will carry out experiments to produce biomass through cultivation of Escherichia coli using batch and chemostat bioreactors. You will investigate how two different modes of cultivation (batch and fed batch) will affect the E. coli growth and biomass yield. You will analyze RNA sequencing- based transcriptome datasets derived from Pseudomonas aeruginosa using a next-generation sequencing (NGS) analysis pipeline. The gained knowledge and skills will be useful for future industrial biotechnological applications and graduate research studies. Course Content: 1. Bioreactor components and functions 2. Bioreactor monitor and control systems (software and hardware) 3. Transcriptomics analysis pipeline and functions
