2018 1 Renaissance Engineering Year 2 (Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering)
CH1104 MATERIALS & ENERGY BALANCES 4.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: CH1004, CH1119
Not available to Programme: CBE(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), CBE(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
This course introduces material and energy balance concepts, calculations and problem solving skills. Material balance for single and multi-component systems. Energy balance on non-reactive/reactive/transient processes. Computer-aided Mass & Energy balance calculations.
 
CH1131 BIOMOLECULAR ENGINEERING 4.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: CH1031
Not available to Programme: CBE(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), CBE(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
The course will cover the basic and functional aspects of cellular systems. Analysis of components and their functionality in cellular systems is included in this course. Students will also be exposed to aspects of biotechnology, genetic and metabolic engineering which play an increasingly important role in the new era of chemical engineering.
 
CH2103 FLUID SYSTEMS 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: CH1104 & CH1117
Mutually exclusive with: CH2003
Not available to Programme: CBE(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), CBE(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
This course introduces fluid behaviours in various systems relevant to industrial practice. Mass, Energy and Momentum Balances related to fluid flows. Fluid flow in pipes. Dimensional analysis, Pump and compressors. Differential equations of fluid mechanics.
 
CH2107 COMPUTATIONAL METHODS IN CHEMICAL ENGINEERING 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: CH1117
Mutually exclusive with: CE1003, CH2007, CV1014, CZ1003
Not available to Programme: CBE(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), CBE(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
Introduction to solving mathematical problems via numerical methods. Introduction to computation algorithms and application of the programming packages. Matrix algebra, Linear algebraic equations, Eigenvalues and eigenvectors, Non-linear polynomial equations, Function approximation, Numerical integration and differentiation, Ordinary differential equations, and Partial differential equations.
 
RE2011 RENAISSANCE DESIGN I 4.0 AU
This course is the first of two parts to an integrated engineering innovate-and-design curriculum which aims to introduce a systematic engineering design process to the students and provide a design project experience to develop their design skills. The course therefore aims to provide students with an understanding of customer needs, product/design specifications, design concept generation, various design methodologies, aesthetics and industrial design, the application of computer-aided tools to the design/evaluation/manufacturing process of products/systems, and a systems approach to design.
 
RE8013 FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: Only offered to Renaissance Engineering students
The objective of this course is to provide students with a broad understanding of the key financial principles, concepts and analytical tools. This is an introductory course in finance. Topics include the time value of money, interest rates, bond and stock valuation, capital budgeting, risk and return, cost of capital, market efficiency, corporate financing and payout policy, and an introduction to options.
