BS0001 BIOLOGY & SOCIETY 3.0 AU
Not available to Programme: BMS(2004-2010), BS(2004-2010)(Non Direct Entry), BS(2004-2011)(Direct Entry)
This course aims to enlighten you about the importance of studying biology and to deliver an unbiased view of the impact of biology on human society. You will study about various ecosystems such as rainforests and mangrove forests and the social and econonomical values they give to mankind. You will learn about some biological discoveries and innovations that are used in our society in the field of forensics and sports. You will learn about the potential benefits and threats that bio-technology, particularly genetic engineering, brings to mankind and the environment. You will learn about viruses that are harmful to man. You will learn the biology of our human body and bacteria that have benefited mankind in improving health and well-being.
 
BS1001 INTRODUCTORY BIOLOGY 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: BS101, BS101A, CM1051, CY1001
Not available to Programme: CHEM
This course aims to introduce biology at a basic molecular and cellular level and brings into perspective the unifying theme of all living things. It presents the relationship between the molecular principles and the macroscopic view of biology. Basic concepts (such as the central dogma, evolution theory) and common biological processes (such as cell division, cellular respiration) will be covered. Current advances in biology that have implications to our society and biology's interaction with other disciplines will be highlighted. This is an online course, with support provided via online discussion and face-to-face informal tutorials.

The aim of this course is to introduce the physical principles that govern chemical systems. It aims to enable students to apply this knowledge to various biological systems with emphasis on biochemical reactions and processes, and demonstrate their understanding of the concepts in biochemical laboratory work. The course will give a solid grounding for understanding and use of biophysical techniques which will be needed in later studies. The course has an emphasis on the mathematical analysis of biological chemistry processes.

ML0001 ABSOLUTE BASICS FOR CAREER 1.0 AU
Grade Type: Pass/Fail
Not available to Programme: ACBS, ACC, BCE, BCG, BUS
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2014)-Direct Entry, (Admyr 2018-onwards)-Non Direct Entry, (Admyr 2019-onwards)-Direct Entry
In today's competitive world, it is essential that students learn the crucial career skills early to ensure a competitive edge sharpened with a heightened sense of workplace values and ethics. This course takes students through a journey of career discovery, understanding and polishing of oneself which will then be translated into job application tools such as resumes and interview skills. All students will have the opportunity to undergo a robust Career Assessment Tool, which assesses their career options and career self and helps them chart a career action plan. They will also learn how to identify and articulate the concept of personal branding and applying that to resumes and interviews and online social media. Students will also be exposed to the basics of grooming to ensure that they are aware of the importance of first impressions in all situations.
