This course examines how financial managers make financing and investment decisions. The course builds on and extends the concepts covered in the basic financial management course. The major topics covered include capital budgeting, capital structure, public offering, short-term finance, merger and acquisitions, financial distress, etc. The analytical tools and financial theories discussed in the course are brought together in practical settings through the discussion of selected cases. Students taking this course should be well-prepared to pursue a career in investment banking, financial analysis, and consulting.
 
BF3211 COMMODITIES TRADING & RISK MANAGEMENT 3.0 AU
To establish a framework for better appreciation of Global Commodity Trading activities, with emphasis on hydrocarbon sector (oil and gas). Focus would be on dynamics in the real world so that participants would be better equipped for further development in the global commodity arenas. Content This course encompasses following salient segments on global commodity trading: A) Basics - Products & Industry Essentials for appreciation of non-technical B) Basics - Market & Trade Essentials C) Market Plus - Essentials for Pricing, Valuation, Trading, & Risk Management Going beyond basics, delving into the real dynamics, methodologies and premise of key benchmarks and their linkages to other markets. D) Real life case studies & Management

The industry seminar series will offer students professional insights and industry perspectives to international trading. The course will serve to link theory with practice, bringing real-world cases and industry practices into the classroom. Students can expect to better appreciate how international trading concepts and principles are applied in different aspects of the trading and commodities sector. Students will also have the opportunity of using financial platforms to analyse data in the commodities and related sectors. This seminar series will: 1. Invite industry specialists in wholesale commodities trading or related products to conduct seminars on their areas of expertise; 2. Train in the use of financial platforms Thomson Reuters Eikon and Bloomberg to access and analyse information and data in commodities and related sectors. 3. Be conducted throughout the academic year based on the timing of the industry specialist seminars.
