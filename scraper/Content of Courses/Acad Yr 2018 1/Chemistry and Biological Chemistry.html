CM4017 BIOMEDICAL IMAGING & SENSING 3.0 CHEM(SPS)
Prerequisite: CM3041
Mutually exclusive with: PH4607
This course will give an overview of the cutting edge medical imaging and bio-sensing techniques currently being developed, by giving special emphasis on the physics fundamentals for various system designs. This course will be divided into two parts. First part will cover the concepts behind photonics based biosensors and its real applications. In the second part, state of the art medical imaging techniques will be covered by introducing its basics followed by instrumentation and its real applications for disease diagnosis and drug discovery. Finally a comparative analysis of these techniques will be discussed
 
CM4043 MOLECULAR MODELLING:PRINCIPLES & APPLICATIONS 3.0 CHEM(SPS)
Prerequisite: By permission
Prerequisite: CBC314 OR
CM3041
Mutually exclusive with: CBC429, CBC921
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
Introduces computational chemistry to synthetic organic chemists. Emphasis is to train students to be able to study organic chemistry related problems by using computational tools. This module is to provide an introduction to some of the techniques used in computational chemistry, and to illustrate how these techniques can be used to study organic chemical phenomena. Topics covered include building molecular structures, studying molecular properties, searching structural conformations and analyzing the thermodynamics and kinetics of organic reactions. The course will be a combination of lectures and hands-on work.
