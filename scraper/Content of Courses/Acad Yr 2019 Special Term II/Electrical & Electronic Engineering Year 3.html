EE3001 ENGINEERING ELECTROMAGNETICS 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: EE2007(Corequisite)
Mutually exclusive with: PH2102
This course aims to develop your understanding of the fundamental principles underlying the theory of electromagnetic fields and waves and your ability to apply this theory to the solution of engineering problems, e.g. solving fields, charges, currents and voltages in electrostatic, magnetostatic and electromagnetic problems. Contents: Static electric and magnetic fields. Maxwell's equations. Wave equation and uniform plane waves. Electromagnetic energy transfer. Reflection of electro-magnetic waves. Transmission lines.

EE3080 DESIGN & INNOVATION PROJECT 2.0 AU
Prerequisite: EEE3 standing
Mutually exclusive with: EE2079, IM3080
The aim of the Design and Innovative Project module is to introduce you to design, prototype and test electrical and electronic engineering projects. It provide you an opportunity to exercise your creative and innovative ideas in a group project environment and excite the imagination of aspiring engineers, innovators and technopreneurs. Contents: Project Charter. Lectures on project management. Design and prototype development. Testing and instrumentation. Project report. Oral Presentation. Demonstration. DIP Competition.
