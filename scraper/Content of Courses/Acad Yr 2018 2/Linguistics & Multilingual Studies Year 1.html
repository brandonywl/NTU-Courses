HG2003 PHONETICS & PHONOLOGY 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: HG203
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
This course introduces students to the study of speech sounds and the analysis of sound systems in the world's languages. Students will develop skills in perceiving, articulating and transcribing vowel and consonant sounds using IPA symbols. They will also be introduced to syllable structures and learn to do phonemic analysis and employ distinctive features and phonological rules to the analysis of sound patterns.
 
HG2010 BILINGUALISM & MULTILINGUALISM 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: HG210
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
This course focuses on issues central to the phenomenon of bilingualism. It evaluates both sociological and psychological evidence and claims about the bilingual experience, and discusses the implications of these claims on the social perception of bilinguals in different societies. Despite the fact that more than half the world?s population is bilingual, the general public still harbours many misconceptions about what it means to be a bilingual. This course examines some of the myths about bilingualism and explores the potential that bilingualism brings to individuals and society.

HG2052 LANGUAGE, TECHNOLOGY & THE INTERNET 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: HG252, HG8003, HG803
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
Like so many other aspects of life, language communication has been revolutionised by the introduction of the Internet. This course explores how the structure and use of English have been shaped by the popularity of new modes of communication made available by the Internet: SMS, e-mail, chatrooms, Internet Relay Chat, Usenet newsgroups, World Wide Web pages, and virtual worlds. HG2093 DISCOVERING UNWRITTEN STORIES 3.0 AU
This course is designed to provide a theoretical frame and language reconstruction training in Toponymy and Toponomastics, both at the level of the historical reconstruction of the origins and original meanings of place names all over the world (in different historical and geographical contexts) and in the evaluation of the relations between Toponymy and human societies. Students will be provided with an overall view on the study of Diachronic Toponymy (undocumented and endangered linguistic contexts) and Historical Toponomastics (linguistic contexts with available historical sources and records) and on their relevance in the reconstruction of proto-languages and in hypothesizing the existence of pre-languages.
 
HG2097 WHAT'S IN A NAME? A GENERAL INTRODUCTION TO ETYMOLOGY 3.0 AU
The Course introduces fundamental topics in Etymology and Historical Onomastics. Key subjects covered include: - basic elements of General Etymology as a Reconstruction Science in Linguistics; - elementary elements of the various branches of Etymological Sciences, Lexicography, Onomastics, and Toponomastics; - comparative readings and exercises ? at an elementary level ? about the subject of the Course, in order that the students can practice directly with Etymological Sciences; - basic elements of Historical Linguistics and Etymology in Historical Onomastics and Historical Toponomastics (Toponymy, Hydronymy, and Oronymy); - direct and experimental application ? at a general, elementary level ? of Historical Phonetics and Historical Laws, case-by-case, to specific cases and samples, mainly focused on some languages of the South-East Asian context; - discussion and debate with students inherent in the topic of the Course
 
HG3010 LANGUAGE & COMMUNICATION DISORDERS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: HG1001
Mutually exclusive with: HG310, HG8010
THIS COURSE INTRODUCES STUDENTS TO BOTH DEVELOPMENTAL AND ACQUIRED LANGUAGE DISORDERS. STUDENTS WILL EXAMINE THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN NORMAL AND ATYPICAL LANGUAGE DEVELOPMENT. LC9101 ADVANCED PRACTICAL CHINESE 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: At least a C6 grade in the GCE'O'level Chinese or equivalent
Not available to Programme: CHIN
This course is designed for students who are already able to speak Mandarin in everyday life situations but are seeking to improve their practical language skills in formal settings, including public, business and professional situations. Through lively authentic teaching materials (print/non-print), such as newspapers, media reports, web resources, and clips of interviews, TV shows and movies, etc., you will be extensively exposed to advanced Chinese language and multiple topics reflecting society, culture and economics. The course will be conducted in a highly interactive mode. You will be motivated to take active participation in carefully designed activities including forum-like tutorials, group discussions, presentations, debates, group project, etc. The course aims to not only train you to be professional in Mandarin listening, speaking, reading and writing skills, but also to cultivate a higher degree of social and cultural sensitivity. The course is organized around the following 5 topics: 1 Brief Introduction of China a Economic geography b History and modern Chinese society 2 Interpersonal relationship 3 Career Development 4 Marketing; Discipline Chinese 5 Sports and entertainment
