HG2003 PHONETICS & PHONOLOGY 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: HG203
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
This course introduces students to the study of speech sounds and the analysis of sound systems in the world's languages. Students will develop skills in perceiving, articulating and transcribing vowel and consonant sounds using IPA symbols. HG2010 BILINGUALISM & MULTILINGUALISM 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: HG210
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2004-2010)
This course focuses on issues central to the phenomenon of bilingualism. It evaluates both sociological and psychological evidence and claims about the bilingual experience, and discusses the implications of these claims on the social perception of bilinguals in different societies. Despite the fact that more than half the world?s population is bilingual, the general public still harbours many misconceptions about what it means to be a bilingual. This course examines some of the myths about bilingualism and explores the potential that bilingualism brings to individuals and society.

HG2093 DISCOVERING UNWRITTEN STORIES 3.0 AU
This course is designed to provide a theoretical frame and language reconstruction training in Toponymy and Toponomastics, both at the level of the historical reconstruction of the origins and original meanings of place names all over the world (in different historical and geographical contexts) and in the evaluation of the relations between Toponymy and human societies. Students will be provided with an overall view on the study of Diachronic Toponymy (undocumented and endangered linguistic contexts) and Historical Toponomastics (linguistic contexts with available historical sources and records) and on their relevance in the reconstruction of proto-languages and in hypothesizing the existence of pre-languages.

The Course introduces fundamental topics in Etymology and Historical Onomastics. Key subjects covered include: - basic elements of General Etymology as a Reconstruction Science in Linguistics; - elementary elements of the various branches of Etymological Sciences, Lexicography, Onomastics, and Toponomastics; - comparative readings and exercises ? at an elementary level ? about the subject of the Course, in order that the students can practice directly with Etymological Sciences; - basic elements of Historical Linguistics and Etymology in Historical Onomastics and Historical Toponomastics (Toponymy, Hydronymy, and Oronymy); - direct and experimental application ? at a general, elementary level ? of Historical Phonetics and Historical Laws, case-by-case, to specific cases and samples, mainly focused on some languages of the South-East Asian context; - discussion and debate with students inherent in the topic of the Course

This is an introductory course to the research methods and basic statistical techniques commonly used in psychology research. Students will be introduced to the process of scientific inquiry in psychology, both in terms of empirical research methodology and statistical analysis. The course is divided into lectures and tutorials. The lectures will focus on conceptual issues and cover the content materials that students need to understand to actually design any psychological study and work with any data. The tutorials will be dedicated to more in-depth discussions of some of the topics covered in the lectures, and practical experience with data analysis using SPSS
