BA2202 MATHEMATICS OF FINANCE 4.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: BU9223, MH3513, MH4514
Not available to Programme: CEE, CVEC, EEE, ENG, MAT, ME(DES), ME(NULL), ME(RMS), MEEC(DES), MEEC(NULL), MEEC(RMS), MTEC
The aim of this course is to equip students with the knowledge of the theory and practical applications of the mathematics of finance in the various aspects of actuarial work. Topics include the theory of rates of interest and discount, the forces of interest and discount, the evaluation of various annuities including mortgages and bonds, derivatives, yield rate, and stochastic interest rate models.
 
BA2203 STATISTICAL MODELLING 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB103 OR
AB1202
Mutually exclusive with: BR2201
This course provides background knowledge in probability and statistics. In particular, it focuses on statistical modelling that is important in the study of actuarial science. The necessary concepts and techniques in statistics and probability as well as the development of underlying theory will be presented. In addition to theory and methods, students are also expected to master problem-solving skills. Students are also encouraged to use a statistical package such as MATLAB, SPSS or SAS.
