SS3001 STATISTICS, MEASUREMENT & EVALUATION 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS2006 OR
ABA206
Mutually exclusive with: ABA206
This course is designed to provide students with basic concepts of measurement and evaluation in sport science and management. In addition, the course will focus on fundamental concepts of statistics and the use of computers for data analysis. Accordingly, the module introduces students to measurement issues in the sport science and management contexts, descriptive statistics, inferential statistics, the research process, the method of science, scientific investigation, the definition and evaluation of a research problem, hypothesis formation, and data collection.
 
SS3101 EXERCISE & SPORT PHYSIOLOGY II 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS1006 OR
ABA106
A lecture/demonstration laboratory course is designed for the study of selected topics of exercise physiology. The principles of nutrition applied to sports performance and exercise will also be included. Emphasis will be placed on energy release and substrate utilization, energy metabolism during exercise, fluid intake and athletic performance, ergogenic aids, vitamins and minerals, the pre-game meal, sports anemia, nutritional considerations for the diabetic individual, lipid metabolism and coronary heart disease.
 
SS3103 SPORTS INJURIES II 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS2007 OR
ABA207
Physical activity is associated with multiple health benefits and enhanced quality of life. Sports participation across populations in the society is on the rise. Moreover, there has been a significant increase in number of adventure sports participants in recent times. The injury risks, patterns, principles of management and preventive strategies are different in each cohort. This course is designed to provide students with knowledge and the understanding of population-specific sports injuries and skills for primary management of injuries and preventive strategies.
 
SS3201 SPORT BIOMECHANICS II 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS2003 OR
ABA203
This course introduces participants to quantitative methods for determining mechanical parameters of movement, namely linear and angular kinematics and kinetics. Through laboratory work, participants will also learn about the operation of the equipment associated with the measuring techniques in sports biomechanics. Participants will be introduced to the theory governing these measurement techniques and studies that use of these measurement techniques. The aim of the course is to enable participants to effectively analyse movement, explain causes of observed effects and to correct errors in physical performance in sports through the use of these quantitative methods. Participants should have completed the mechanical and anatomical bases of human motion before enrolling for this course.

SS3501 SOCIOLOGY OF SPORT II 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: SS1002 OR
ABA102
The theoretical contributions of Durkheim, Weber and the Marxists provide the backdrop for a study of the significance of sport within society during the twentieth and twenty first centuries. Themes and issues relating to the expansion of mass and elite sport are examined within local and international settings. Eastern and western perspectives of the significance of sport and physical activity are compared. Power relations and the accommodation of various groups within the sporting system are considered.
 
SS3601 SPORT MARKETING & SPONSORSHIP 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: BU8541, SS2104
Not available to all Programme with: (Admyr 2016-onwards)-Non Direct Entry
The course on sport marketing and sponsorship investigates the nature of sport products and the motivations of sport consumers. The sport consumer types are identified and their behaviour discussed. The essentials of marketing will be taught and related to sport. Students of the course will be given opportunity to work on a sport marketing project to appreciate and reinforce the complexity and dynamics of sport marketing. Sponsorship issues and strategies will also be covered including the relationship of sponsors to the sport, platforms and benefits of sport sponsorships.
 
SS3602 EVENT & FACILITIES MANAGEMENT 3.0 AU
Sport is often presented through events which can be major meets like the World Cup or Olympics or regional and local events like a school meet. Students will through this course understand the importance of sport events and the decision making considerations of hosting an event. Opportunities will also be provided for students to experientially learn event management. Important to any sport event are the sport facilities which require strict compliance to specifications and maintenance. The concept of a multi-functional sport facility will also be explored. Risk management during events and in facilities management will be highlighted.
 
SS3603 HUMAN RESOURCE MANAGEMENT IN SPORT 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: AE4008, MP4008
Managing human resources well is vital to any organisation. Students of this course will first begin to understand the different functions of human resource management such as recruiting, inducting, developing, and assessing personnel of the organisation. An important human resource asset to most sport organisation are volunteers. The motivation to participate in sport and the strategies of retaining sport volunteers will also be discussed and explored.
 
SS3604 LEGAL ASPECTS IN SPORT 3.0 AU
Managing sport requires developing relationships between organisations, and between individuals representing the sport and organisation. Students of sport management must have an understanding of the legal aspects of these relationships and the roles and responsibilities of sport officials. Topics of this course include sources of law, duty of care of sport officials, the concept of tort and negligence, contracts, and others. Sport tribunals and its conduct will also be covered. Relevant case studies will be used for discussion and learning.
