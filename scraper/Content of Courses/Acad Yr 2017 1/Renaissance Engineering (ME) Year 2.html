MA2012 INTRODUCTION TO MECHATRONICS SYSTEMS DESIGN 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: MA8002
Not available to Programme: ME(DES), ME(NULL)
The lecture parts of course will introduce the concept of mechatronics and the elements of mechatronics system or product. It will also dwell on the basics of motors, sensors, methods of interfacing them to an embedded processor, and the various forms of inputs and output devices that allow for human interface in addition to automation. Tutorials are to be conducted in laboratory where students are given exercises to implement and practiced. It involves the use of bread boards for development of interfacing circuits and inputs and output devices. Students are required to complete a group project where they are to design, fabricate and demonstrate your product to the class.

This course introduces mechanics skills in analysing deformation and failure of structures and materials. It introduces some advanced topics in mechanics, such as energy methods, fracture and fatigue. RE2003 MECHANICAL & STRUCTURAL ENGINEERING II 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: RE1003
The purpose of a mechanism is to transmit motion and force. This course introduces the students some basic mechanisms including linkages, cams and gears. Mechanisms are the building blocks of a machine. In a machine design problem, very often the first task is to determine the type and configurations of mechanisms to provide the desired motions. Once the kinematic issues are addressed, the dynamic analysis can then be performed. In this course, techniques for the kinematic and dynamic analyses of mechanisms will be covered. A course in the theory of mechanisms is essential for any mechanical engineer as the knowledge is fundamental in understanding the working of machines. Apart from analytical ability, it also provides students the opportunities to develop their design capability. On structural analysis of trusses, beams and frames, the course covers the determination of deflection curve using the energy methods and principle of virtual work. On vibration of structural systems, it covers the equations of motion of single degree and multiple degrees of freedom systems, effects of damping, and the structural response to ground excitations.

RE8005 FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: Only offered to Renaissance Engineering students
The objective of this course is to provide students with a broad understanding of the key financial principles, concepts and analytical tools. This is an introductory course in finance. Topics include the time value of money, interest rates, bond and stock valuation, capital budgeting, risk and return, cost of capital, market efficiency, corporate financing and payout policy, and an introduction to options.
