ES2004 LAYERS & LANDFORMS 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: ES1003
This course introduces students to process geomorphology, sedimentology and stratigraphic principles, and provides tools for the interpretation of sediments and sedimentary rocks and basins. Lectures in weeks 1-2 cover sediments composition, sediment transport and sedimentary structures. Lecture in week 3 introduces the facies approach to sedimentology and geomorphology. The second phase moves to conceptualise the processes of formation, transport and deposition of sediment. During lectures in weeks 5-8 students develop conceptual models for the full range of landscapes and sedimentary environments on earth, from desert dunes to deep seas muds and coral reefs to gravel bed rivers. Lectures from weeks 8-11 introduce the concepts of sequence stratigraphy and sedimentary basin analysis. The lecture series is supplemented by 8 laboratory exercises and 2 field trips the first a half day and the second a 4 day trip to Miri in Malaysia. The field program is a major part of the course both in content and assessment.

ES3003 INTRODUCTION TO GEOCHEMISTRY 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: ES1003
This course is designed to introduce students the principles of chemistry related to geology and their applications to understand processes taking place on and within the Earth. By the end of the course, students should become familiar with the basic geochemical tools, and be able to use these tools to solve, in a quantitative manner, some geological and environmental problems, for example, measuring geological time, unravelling the continental and oceanic evolutions, and understanding the current climate change. Content We will investigate large scale processes occurring in the geosphere, hydrosphere, biosphere, atmosphere and anthroposphere using major and trace element geochemistry and stable isotopes. We will discuss processes such as element and isotope fractionation, element transport and mixing as well as radiogenic isotopes and their application for geochronological purposes. Hands-on data collection and analysis will be core to the students learning experience and will be done during tutorials and assignments.
 
ES3101 PETROLEUM GEOLOGY OF SE ASIA 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: ES2004 & ES3002
This course is divided into 3 parts. In Part (i) students will be introduced to the Petroleum System i.e. from source to reservoir for both conventional and unconventional oil and gas. They will learn about siliciclastic and limestone source and reservoir rocks, thermal maturation during burial, porosity and permeability during migration and sealing, and seismic interpretation of structural and stratigraphic traps. Drilling, logging, appraisal and production methods will be outlined. In Part (ii) students will study the geological settings of the oil and gas fields in Malaysia, Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, Thailand, Cambodia, Myanmar, Philippines, Vietnam and southern China. Students will prepare and make group presentations on a world class petroleum province and apply this knowledge to planning an exploration programme in SE Asia. In Part (iii) students will undertake a 5-day field excursion (during the mid-semester break) to an active petroleum system in South East Asia (e.g. Labuan). It is expected that participants will become Student Members (free of charge) of the South East Asian Exploration Society and attend the bi-monthly meetings in Singapore. Here, they will be able to mix with working petroleum explorers and discuss internships and career prospects.
