HW0288 ENGINEERING COMMUNICATION 2.0 AU

Prerequisite: HW0188 (Not Applicable to IEM)

Not available to Programme: AERO 1, AERO 2, ASEC 1, ASEC 2, BEEC 1, BEEC 2, BIE 1, BIE 2, CBE 1, CBE 2, CBEC 1, CBEC 2, CE 1, CE 2, CEE 1, CEE 2, CEEC 1, CEEC 2, CSC 1, CSC 2, CSEC 1, CSEC 2, CVEC 1, CVEC 2, EEE 2, ENE 1, ENE 2, ENEC 1, ENEC 2, IEM 2, MAT 1, MAT 2, ME(DES) 2, ME(NULL) 2, ME(RMS) 2, MEEC(DES) 2, MEEC(NULL) 2, MEEC(RMS) 2, MS(2019-onwards), MTEC 1, MTEC 2
HW0288 is an advanced course which aims to enhance the spoken and written abilities of engineering students when communicating in both academic and professional settings. In this course, the emphasis is on advanced skills in academic and professional communication. In the academic literacy and information literacy components of the course, you learn advanced skills for searching academic databases, evaluating sources and incorporating and citing them appropriately in your writing. In the professional communication component of the course, you learn written and oral workplace communication skills such as writing emails, participating at meetings, interacting with clients and projecting a professional image of yourself.
 
MS3011 METALLIC & CERAMIC MATERIALS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: MS2016(Corequisite)
Mutually exclusive with: MS3001
Introduction and classification of engineering metals and alloys. Strengthening mechanisms. Microstructure and heat treatment of ferrous alloys. Mechanical properties of ferrous alloys. Non Ferrous alloys. Engineering ceramics. Functional ceramics.
 
MS3012 MICRO/NANOELECTRONIC MATERIALS PROCESSING 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: MS3002
Microelectronics Technology Overview. Semiconductor Device Physics. Bulk Crystal Growth. Diffusion and Thermal Oxidation. Ion Implantation & Simulation. Thin Film Deposition. Patterning. Advanced Lithography. Advanced Deposition Techniques. Advanced Devices
 
MS3013 ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS ON MATERIALS 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: MS2013(Corequisite) & MS3011(Corequisite)
Mutually exclusive with: MS3003
This course introduces environmental effects on materials performance Effects on metal, ceramic and polymer based materials. Prevention of materials degradation induced by environment
 
MS3014 ANALYSIS OF MATERIALS 3.0 AU
Mutually exclusive with: MS3007
Not available to Programme: ENG, ME(DES), ME(NULL), ME(RMS)
This course introduces a broad range of characterization techniques in the following areas: 1. Chemical Analysis 2. Spectroscopy Techniques 3. Basic Imaging Techniques 4. Basic Crystal Structure Analysis 5. Physical Properties Analysis
 
MS3015 MATERIALS ASPECTS IN DESIGN 3.0 AU
Prerequisite: MS1015 & MS2013(Corequisite) & MS3011(Corequisite)
Mutually exclusive with: MS3005
This course introduces theory of design in an intermediate to advanced level. 1. Principle of design process, motivation and translation. 2. Develop charts to rank materials according the objective. Use charts to obtain the global overview of engineering materials and develop strategy in the design of new materials. 3. A 10 week group design project on materials-related topics

MS4613 WEARABLE SENSORS FOR HEALTHCARE 3.0 AU

MS4663 ENGINEERING SOLUTIONS BY DESIGN THINKING 3.0 AU
Through participation in projects involving real human issues, you will experience how the concepts, methods, and sensibilities of design can complement the knowledge, skills, and values of material science engineering to create innovative solutions to complex human problems that are dynamic, multi-perspectival, and multi-stable, and have no unique or determinate solution.
